## The 4 Dimensions of Enterprise AI Readiness

Research across 500+ enterprise AI transformations identifies four critical dimensions that determine success:

### 1. Technology (T-Score)
Your infrastructure, tools, and data quality foundation.

**Indicators of strong Technology readiness:**
- Standardized AI tools approved and deployed organization-wide
- Quality data available and accessible for AI use cases
- Integration between AI tools and core business systems
- Clear data governance and data dictionary

**Common Technology gaps and remedies:**
- **Gap: No standardized tools** → Remedy: Pilot one AI tool with 10 users, measure ROI, then scale
- **Gap: Poor data quality** → Remedy: Prioritize data cleaning for top 3 AI use cases before anything else
- **Gap: No integration** → Remedy: Use Power Automate as the connective tissue — 200+ connectors, no code required

**Quick win for Technology-lagging organizations:**
Deploy Microsoft Copilot in Teams first. Every meeting gets summaries, action items, and follow-ups automatically. Immediate, visible ROI. Takes 15 minutes to configure.

---

### 2. People (P-Score)
Your team's AI skills, mindset, and change readiness.

**Indicators of strong People readiness:**
- Most employees use AI tools weekly in their core work
- At least one "AI Champion" per team
- Leadership actively models AI adoption
- Psychological safety to experiment and fail

**Common People gaps and remedies:**
- **Gap: Fear of AI replacing jobs** → Remedy: Reframe AI as "the junior colleague who does drafts" — humans still lead, judge, and decide
- **Gap: Inconsistent skill levels** → Remedy: Create peer learning pairs — your AI Champions mentor 3 colleagues each
- **Gap: No time to learn** → Remedy: Replace one weekly meeting with a 30-minute AI show-and-tell session

**Learning investment ROI:**
Organizations that invest in structured AI training see 3.2x faster adoption and 47% higher utilization of AI tools vs. organizations that rely on self-discovery.

---

### 3. Process (R-Score)
How AI is integrated into your documented, repeatable workflows.

**Indicators of strong Process readiness:**
- AI tasks are documented in standard operating procedures
- Feedback loops exist to improve AI outputs over time
- Clear handoff points between AI and human decision-makers
- ROI is measured on at least 3 AI use cases

**Common Process gaps and remedies:**
- **Gap: AI used informally but not in processes** → Remedy: Map your top 5 processes, identify where AI fits, add it to the SOP
- **Gap: No measurement** → Remedy: Use the "10% rule" — track time spent before and after AI on any task, even informally
- **Gap: AI outputs not reviewed** → Remedy: Add a "human review" step to every AI output workflow — 2 minutes prevents big mistakes

**Process maturity stages:**
1. Ad hoc → 2. Piloting → 3. Standardized → 4. Optimized → 5. AI-native

Most organizations sit at Stage 2. Moving to Stage 3 (standardized) delivers 60% of the total value.

---

### 4. Governance (G-Score)
Your policies, ethics frameworks, and accountability structures.

**Indicators of strong Governance readiness:**
- Written AI usage policy that all employees have acknowledged
- AI use case registry tracking what AI is used for what purpose
- Responsible AI review process before new AI deployments
- Clear escalation path for AI ethics concerns

**Common Governance gaps and remedies:**
- **Gap: No AI policy** → Remedy: Start with Microsoft's Responsible AI Standard as your template — adapt it in a 2-hour workshop
- **Gap: No AI inventory** → Remedy: Create a simple SharePoint list: Tool | Use Case | Owner | RAI Status
- **Gap: No escalation path** → Remedy: Designate one "AI Ethics Owner" per department — route all concerns there first

**Why governance matters financially:**
Organizations without AI governance policies are 4x more likely to experience an AI-related incident that causes reputational or financial damage.

## The AI Transformation Journey — 5 Stages

| Stage | Characteristics | Typical Score | Key Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Aware | Know AI exists, haven't started | 0-20% | Education, leadership buy-in |
| 2. Experimenting | Trying tools, no structure | 21-40% | Standardize one tool, measure ROI |
| 3. Adopting | Regular use, some processes | 41-60% | Document processes, train everyone |
| 4. Scaling | AI in core workflows | 61-80% | Governance, centers of excellence |
| 5. AI-Native | AI is default, humans focus on judgment | 81-100% | Innovation, frontier AI |

---

## 30/60/90 Day Framework for Each Stage

### Stage 1-2 (Score 0-40%): Foundation Phase

**Day 1-30 (Quick Wins):**
- Deploy one AI tool to the whole team
- Complete AI fundamentals training (all staff)
- Identify 3 AI use cases with clear ROI potential
- Designate one AI Champion per team

**Day 31-60 (Build Habits):**
- Run AI use case pilots on the top 3 opportunities
- Measure and document results
- Create a simple AI usage policy (1 page)
- Weekly AI win sharing session

**Day 61-90 (Embed & Scale):**
- Document AI workflows in SOPs
- Expand to second wave of use cases
- Set up basic AI metrics dashboard
- First governance review

### Stage 3-4 (Score 41-80%): Scale Phase

**Day 1-30:**
- Establish AI Center of Excellence
- Create formal AI use case pipeline
- Implement Responsible AI review process

**Day 31-60:**
- Advanced AI training for technical staff
- AI integration in at least 50% of key processes
- ROI dashboard for leadership

**Day 61-90:**
- Cross-functional AI projects
- AI strategy presentation to board/leadership
- Contribution to industry AI communities

## Key Metrics to Track

| Metric | How to Measure | Target |
|---|---|---|
| AI Tool Adoption Rate | % of employees using AI tools weekly | >70% by Month 3 |
| Time Saved | Hours saved per employee per week via AI | >2 hours/week |
| Use Case ROI | Value generated by top AI use cases | 3x investment |
| Training Completion | % of team completing AI training | 100% basics, 50% advanced |
| AI Incidents | RAI-related issues reported | 0 high-severity |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Starting with technology before culture** — Tools without adoption = shelfware
2. **Skipping governance** — Speed now = incident risk later
3. **One-size-fits-all training** — Engineers and executives need different learning paths
4. **No measurement** — "We're using AI" is not a success metric
5. **Forgetting change management** — 60% of AI resistance is about fear, not capability
